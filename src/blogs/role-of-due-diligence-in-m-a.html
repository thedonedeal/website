Diligence is where deals are either confirmed or quietly killed. Understanding what buyers check — and preparing for it — is the founder's best leverage.

In the Indian startup ecosystem, diligence has become more rigorous as capital has become more professional. The good news: it's entirely survivable when you've prepared.

What buyers actually examine

  • Financial — quality of revenue and earnings, not just totals
  • Legal — contracts, IP ownership, cap table, compliance
  • Commercial — customer concentration, retention, pipeline
  • People — key-person risk and team continuity

Prepare before you're asked

The fastest diligence is the one where every answer already exists. Founders who keep a clean data room and honest metrics turn diligence from a threat into a trust-builder.

Diligence rarely changes a buyer's mind. It confirms what your cue card already told them — for better or worse.
